High Floors (Top / Upper Floors)

• Premium views (skyline, greenery, cityscape)

• Less noise, dust (especially near busy roads)

• More privacy, better ventilation & sunlight

• Higher resale value in luxury projects

Concerns:

• Higher temperature in summers (roof heat).

• Dependency on lifts (power backup is a must).

• Higher maintenance cost sometimes.

Middle Floors

• Balanced choice — neither too high nor too low.

• Easy accessibility without being too dependent on elevators.

• Reasonable sunlight & ventilation.

• Lower price compared to top floors, but still better than ground floors in terms of dust/noise.

Concerns:

• Not as premium as top floors, but more expensive than ground floors.

• Noise from surrounding flats.

Lower Floors (Ground + 1st–2nd Floor)

• Easy accessibility (can avoid lifts).

• Faster evacuation in case of emergency.

• Convenient for carrying groceries, strollers, or furniture.

• Sometimes come with extra features like garden/terrace (in luxury apartments).

Concerns:

• More dust, insects, and traffic noise (especially near expressways like Noida-Greater Noida).

• Less sunlight and ventilation.

• Lower resale value compared to higher floors.
